Nachos




Nachos - in my humble and fatty opinion - are the ultimate comfort food because, at the end of the day you're just piling a bunch of your favorite food on top of a bunch of your favorite chips with as much queso, sour cream and other nonsense sauces you want and diving in face first like a freakin' animal.

While there are a lot of different ways to make amazing nachos, we'll focus on a classic ground beef recipe that will make you only consider Taco Bell when you're heavily under the influence and unsafe to operate a stove.

Ingredients

Amount Unit Ingredient
bags Tortilla Chips
lb(s) Ground Beef
cups Milk
tsp. Kosher Salt
tbsp. Vegetable Oil
tbsp. Flour
cup(s) Cheddar and Monterey Cheese
tbsp. Sour Cream
cup(s) Black Beans

Servings:


Method

  1. Let's start with the queso! Heat the oil in a small sauce pan over medium-low heat for 1-2 minutes and then add the flour and mix until combined. You want a thick paste-like consistency, so add more oil or flour as necessary in very small amounts until you get that consistency.
  2. Add the milk in small amounts and whisk in to the mixture until it heats up. It will thicken as it heats and once it is mixed even add more milk until it is all added.
  3. At this point you can add some seasonings like salt, garlic powder, actual garlic, whatever. I just didn't feel like detailing that.
  4. Once the milk is warm and seasoned, turn the heat to low and start adding your cheese in small batches and stir with a rubber spatula so you can scrape the bottom and sides of the pan. Once all the chese is melted you have queso! You can either continue or just get lazy, grab your bag of chips and eat all the cheese now.
  5. For the beef, pre-heat a skillet on medium heat with a touch of oil to barely coat the bottom of the pan and slap your beef in there (or set gently if your a sissy about oil burns) and spread and press it down so as much of the beef is touching the pan as possible (brown that beef!).
  6. Again, add seasonings here if you want, salt, pepper, garlic, chilli powder, mint leaves, brown sugar, espresso powder - mix that shit up.
  7. Stir beef until it is all brown and then take off heat.
  8. Finally, assemble! Put all your chips on a plate and make sure they are distributed evenly so that chips only barely cover each other and the entire bottom of the plate is covered. You will look like a sociopath while you do this (but that's just cuz people don't understand, they NEVER understand...), but it will ensure the perfect bit everytime. If you want more chips, just add a second layer. You cannot have too many nacho layers.
  9. Add the queso next (if you haven't eaten it all by now) and then a generous - and evenly distributed for the love of god - portion of all of your toppings, all of it, everything, put everything on here.
  10. One extra step if you want to be awesome is to do the assembly in a cast-iron pan and throw that pan in a 400 degree oven for a few minutes to get everything warm and crispy.
